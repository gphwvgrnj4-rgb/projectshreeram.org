Frequently Asked Questions

Straight answers about who we are, what we do, and how the official pathways actually work.

Can Project Shree Ram get me a job in Israel?
No. We are a cultural and advocacy nonprofit, not a recruiter or placement agency. We point you to the official government portals, but we play no part in selection, contracts, or travel.
Do you charge any fees?
Never. Everything we publish is free. If anyone claiming to represent us asks you for money, it is not us — and it is a scam.
How do I actually apply to work in Israel?
Through the official Government of India / Government of Israel portals listed on our Official Pathways page. That is the only legitimate route.
Which sectors are hiring?
The government-to-government framework has covered sectors such as elder care, agriculture, and construction. The official portals list current openings.
Is it safe? How do I avoid being cheated?
Deal only with the official portals. No genuine process guarantees a job or visa for a payment, and official portals never charge application fees. See Official Pathways for the full safety checklist.
What is Ger Toshav?
A Torah concept of a righteous non-Jewish resident who lives among the Jewish people under a covenant of mutual respect and dignity. We see it as a fitting frame for the Hindu–Jewish friendship. More on our About page.
What is the petition?
A public call, signed by Indian and Israeli citizens, asking both governments to expand the existing legal pathways between the two countries. You can read and sign it on the home page.
How do you use my information?
Only to respond to you. See our Privacy Policy. We never sell your data and never pass it to any government agency.

Project Shree Ram is an independent Hindu–Jewish cultural and advocacy nonprofit. We are not a government body, a staffing agency, or a placement company; we process no job applications and charge no fees.

All labor applications, selection, contracts, and travel between India and Israel are handled entirely by the two governments and their authorized agencies. Beware of anyone charging fees or promising jobs — official government portals never do.
